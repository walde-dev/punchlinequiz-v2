## Design Context

### Users
German hip hop fans, age 16–30. Using the app on their phone during downtime — commuting, between classes, late at night. They know the culture deeply and want to prove it. The job to be done is: **flex rap knowledge, beat friends, feel like the real one in the room.** They came from a Reddit post or a WhatsApp share card, which means someone they know already played — social proof is baked into every arrival.

### Brand Personality
**Competitive, Cultural, Slick.**

The tone is a skilled trash talker who respects the culture — like a friend who roasts you but buys the next round. Not mean, not friendly. The energy is: *"You should know this. Let's see."* Think Wordle's clean simplicity with Duolingo's competitive hooks and Spotify's dark immersive canvas — filtered through German hip hop attitude.

Emotional goal: users feel **pride when they get it right** and **urgency to redeem themselves when they don't**. Wrong answers sting just enough to make you try again.

Copy examples:
- Correct: "Ehre. Du kennst das." / "Respekt."
- Wrong (3 fails): "Nicht mal nah dran. Hier ist die Lösung."
- Score reveal: "7/10. Nicht schlecht. Aber Kollegah-Fan bist du damit nicht."

### Aesthetic Direction
**Dark Spotify canvas with gold crown energy.**

- **Background:** Deep charcoal blacks, #121212–#1f1f1f. Never white or light.
- **Accent:** Gold #fbbf24 — ONE accent, used consistently. Correct answers, CTAs, highlights, streaks. Never diluted with secondary accent colors.
- **Text:** White (#ffffff) for primary, #b3b3b3 for muted/metadata, #7c7c7c for disabled/hints.
- **Font:** Figtree Variable — geometric, modern, confident. Bold weights for punchline text and display. Regular for body.
- **Buttons:** Pill-shaped (9999px radius), gold primary, ghost for secondary actions.
- **Cards:** #181818 background, subtle border #2e2e2e, heavy shadow for elevation.
- **Visuals:** Album art and artist photos are the visuals — no stock illustrations, no decorative icons.

References: Spotify (dark canvas), Wordle (shareable score cards), Duolingo (streaks, competitive hooks), Discord (gaming community dark UI).

Anti-references: Light backgrounds, serif fonts, multiple accent colors, glassmorphism, school quiz UI, any "fun learning" aesthetic.

### Design Principles

1. **The punchline is the hero.** Every screen exists to serve the lyric. Text is the visual. UI chrome recedes into darkness, content glows.

2. **One accent, everywhere consistently.** Gold (#fbbf24) only. Never add a second accent. Never use blue, green, or purple as a design element. Semantic states (wrong = red #f3727f, streak = orange #ffa42b) are exceptions with specific purpose.

3. **Competitive but not cruel.** Copy has edge and attitude but never punches the user — it punches the *lack of knowledge*. The game respects anyone who plays; it just demands you prove yourself.

4. **Share-first design.** Every completed round state should feel worth screenshotting. The share card is the growth engine. Design every score/result moment as if it will be the first thing a new user sees on WhatsApp.

5. **Speed over spectacle.** Animations serve feedback, not decoration. A correct answer gets confetti. A wrong answer shakes the input. Navigation is instant. Never delay a game interaction for an animation. Keep it snappy.
